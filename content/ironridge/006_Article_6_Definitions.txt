Article 6. Definitions
a.	Home Occupation: A small business operated from within a home with no 
customers visiting.
b.	Setback: Required space between a building and a property line; limits how large a 
home can be.
c.	Buffer: A landscaped strip that separates different types of land uses.
d.	Special Use Permit: A case-by-case approval process that may be denied based on 
parking, traffic, or neighborhood compatibility.
e.	Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU): A small, secondary housing unit (like a basement 
apartment or backyard cottage).
f.	Lot Coverage: The percentage of a lotâ€™s total area that is covered by buildings or 
other structures, including the main building, garages, and accessory structures. Lot 
coverage regulations limit how much of a property can be built on, ensuring open 
space for yards, landscaping, drainage, and overall neighborhood character.
